Nikola Tesla (1856-1943)

The man who invented Alternating Current (AC)

The inventor of alternating current, Nikola Tesla

1856: Tesla's Birthday
Nikola Tesla was born to a Serbian family in Smiljan in Lika in the Military Frontier of the Austrian Empire near the Ottoman border. His father, Milutin, was a Serbian Orthodox priest and mother, Đuka Mandić, was talented and made home craft tools and mechanical appliances.

1861: Tesla Attends School
Tesla attended his first school year in Smiljan, where he studied German, arithmetic, and religion

1862: Tesla Family Moves to Gospić
Tesla's farther receives appointment as pastor to a church in Gospić. Tesla completed primary school, followed by middle school in Gospić.

1863: Tesla's Brother Dies
Death of older and only brother Dane at age 12 after falling from a horse. Some accounts indicate Tesla caused the accident by spooking the horse.

1870: Tesla Moves to Karlovac
Tesla moves to Karlovac (Carlstadt), Croatia and completes his high school at the "higher Real Gymnasium" where teacher Martin Sekulić teaches him math and physics and has a decisive influence on him. Tesla graduates a year early.

1873: Tesla Contracts Cholera
Tesla returns to Gospić an shortly after arriving he contracts Cholera. He is bedridden for 9 months and was near death many times. Tesla's father agreed to send him to an engineering school if he recovered from the illness.

1874: Tesla Eludes the Army Service
Tesla went to Tomingaj near Gračac to recover from Cholera and to evade conscription into the Austro-Hungarian Army in Smiljan. He explored the mountains and said tht this contact with nature made him stronger, both physically and mentally.

1875: Tesla Attends College in Graz
Tesla enrolled at the Imperial-Royal Technical College in Graz on a Military Frontier scholarship. In his first year Tesla never missed a lecture, earned the highest grades possible, passed nine exams, (nearly twice as many as required) and started a Serv cultural club. But his third year he was failing in school nad never graduated, leaving Graz.

1878: Tesla Moves to Maribor
Tesla choose Maribor, Slovenia as his next stop. He took his first job as a draftsman for a local engineering firm. Tesla spent his spare time in a playing chess and cards.

1879: Tesla Deported from Maribor and Teaches in Gospić
In March Tesla' father beg his son to return home but he refused. Later Tesla was reported to Maribor police because he did not have a residence permit. Tesla was returned to Gospić under police guard. His father died at the age of 60. Tesla taught a large class of students in his old school in Gospić, but did not want this to b his future.

1880: Tesla Arrives in Prague
Two of Tesla's uncles came to his rescue and put together enough money to help him leave Gospić. Tesla travelled to Prague, but arrive too late to enroll at Charles-Ferdinand University. He also had never taken Greek and was illiterate in Czech which were required subjects. Tesla did attend lectures at the University as an auditor.

1881: Tesla Works in Budapest
Tesla moved to Budapest, Hungary to work for Tivadar Puskás at a telegraph company, the Budapest telephone Exchange.The company was under construction. He worked as a draftsman and within a few months it became functional, and tesla was allocated the chief electrician position. Tesla made improvements to equipment and developed an amplifier device.

1882: Tesla Takes Job in Paris
Tesla accepted a position with the Continental Edison Company. He gained a great deal of practical experience in electrical engineering. Management took notice of his advanced knowledge and soon had him designing and building improved version of generating dynamos and motors.
