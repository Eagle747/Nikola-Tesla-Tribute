Nikola Tesla (1856-1943)

The man who invented Alternating Current (AC)

The inventor of alternating current, Nikola Tesla

1856: Tesla's date of birth: 10 July 1856.

1861: Tesla attends school in Smiljan, Austrian Empire.

1862: Tesla's family moves to Gospić.

1863: Tesla's older brother, Dane, dies at age 12.

1870: Tesla moves to Karlovac(Carlstadt) and completes high school.

1873: Tesla contracts Cholera and is bedridden for 9 months.

1874: Tesla recovers, eluding army service by staying in the mountains.

1875: Tesla attends college in Graz.

1878: Tesla moves to Maribor, Slovenia.

1879: Tesla is deported from Maribor to Gospić and shortly afterwards his father dies. Tesla teaches in Gospić.

1880: Tesla arrives in Prague and attends lectures as an auditor.

1881: Tesla works at the Budapest Telephone Exchange in Budapest.

1882: Tesla has an epiphany about A.C. and accepts a position at the Continental Edison Company.

1883: On orders of the Continental Edison Company, Tesla went to Strasbourg, France. Tesla demonstrates his A.C. motor to potential investors.

1884: On June 6th, Tesla arrives in New York and meets Edison.

1885: Tesla started the Tesla Electric Light Company in Rahway, New Jersey; Tesla quits Edison's company.

1886: Betrayed by the men Tesla trusted, he went through a winter of suffering.

1887: Tesla Electric Company begins. Szigeti, Tesla's oldest and best friend, lands in New York and starts working as Tesla's assistant.

1888: "A New System of Alternate-Current Motors and Transformers" was demonstrated to the American Institute of Electrical Engineers (AIEE, now IEEE). Tesla sells A.C. patents to George Westinghouse and travels to the headquarters of Westinghouse in Pittsburgh.

1889: Tesla opens new laboratory in Grand street, New York, and chose the Astor House for his new abode. Tesla returns to Europe and visits his homeland.

1890: At the end of 1890 was the beginning of Tesla's lifelong obsession - the wireless transmission of energy. Tesla forfeits A.C. Royalties to Westinghouse. Tesla Opens 5th Avenue lab.

1891: Tesla lectures before AIEE. Westinghouse installs the Tesla A.C. power system below Gold King Mine known as the first power station. Tesla becomes an American citizen. Tesla suspects the existence of charged particles. The Tesla coil is developed.

1892: Tesla lectures in London and Paris. Tesla's mother dies. He lectures in Zagreb and speaks in Belgrade. Back in New York he moves to Hotel Gerlach and elected vice president of the AIEE.

1893: Tesla lectures in Philadelphia and St. Louis. Builds a power system for the Columbian Exposition with the Westinghouse Company. The company is awarded the initial Niagara Falls contract. Tesla lectures in Chicago.

1894: Tesla is voted corresponding member of the Serbian Royal Academy of Belgrade. The first book on his work, "The inventions, researches and writings of Nikola Tesla", is published.

1895: Tesla's 5th Avenue lab burns down. The first large generator of the Niagara Falls Power Plant proved to be quite satisfactory. Tesla creates a picture he called "shadowgraphs" and send pictures to Röntgen.

1896: Tesla and others tour Niagara Falls and the new Adams Power Station which powered Buffalo, New York, 22 miles away. Tesla was elected honorary member of the Yugoslav Academy of Sciences and Arts in Zagreb.

1897: Tesla lectures in Buffalo and New York.

1898: Tesla publicly demonstrates his "automation" technology by wirelessly controlling a model boat.

1899: Tesla arrives in Colorado Springs to continue high-voltage/high-frequency experiments. Terrestrial waves were observed by Tesla. Tesla moves into the Waldorf-Astoria upon receiving an investment. One night Tesla observes strange rhythmic pulses and concludes that creatures from another planet communicated with Earth.

1900: Travelling to Washington, D.C., Tesla discusses his wireless telegraphy system. Tesla patented a corona discharge ozone generator and went into production at the Tesla Ozone Company.

1903: Testing at the Wardenclyffe Tower begins.

1904: The Colorado Springs power company sues Tesla for electricity used and Tesla's Colorado laboratory is torn down.

1906: Tesla announces his bladeless turbine to the world. Tesla is forced to lay employees off at Wardenclyffe. Tesla builds the world's first and only air-friction speedometer.

1907: Tesla is elected as an active member of the New York Academy of Sciences. Tesla opens a office in Broadway and begins work on other inventions.

1910: Tesla gains some initial success with his turbines and relocates the office to the Metropolitan Tower.

1911: Tesla tests numerous models of the steam turbines at the Waterside Station of the New Your Edison Company.

1914: Tesla moves his office from Metropolitan tower to Woolworth Building, then the world's tallest building.

1915: Newspapers stated that Tesla and Edison were to share the Nobel Prize in physics but it was awarded to Professor William Henry Bragg and his son. Tesla relocates to 40th street directly across the street from Bryant Park and his beloved pigeons.

1916: Tesla falls deeper and deeper into debt primarily due to his high overhead expenses.

1917: Tesla receives the Edison Medal. Tesla contracts with Pyle National on the perfection of his turbines. Tesla envisions radar.

1919:"Electrical Experimenter" magazine begins a series of articles entitled "My Inventions" by Tesla.

1922: Tesla's favourite pigeon dies, which he described as being a beautiful female bird, pure white with light grey tips on its wings.

1925: Tesla closes his office for good.

1926: Tesla receives a diploma of honorary doctorate from the Faculty of Engineering, University of Belgrade and from the University of Zagreb.

1930: Tesla relocates to the Hotel Governor Clinton.

1931: Tesla receives many congratulatory letters from prominent scientists on his 75th birthday. "Time" magazine honour the great inventor by placing his portrait on the cover.

1934: Westinghouse Corporation provided Tesla with a consulting rate which covered his monthly rent expenses. Upon signing the agreement, Tesla moves to the Hotel New Yorker. The City of Philadelphia awards Nikola Tesla with the John Scott Medal.

1937: Tesla is awarded the Certificate of honorary doctorate form the University of Poitiers. He was the first scientist nominated for permanent membership of the Serbian Royal Academy of Sciences. Tesla was presented the Grand Cordon of the White Lion. Tesla was struck by a taxi cab while crossing the street to feed his pigeons in the park. The University of Paris awarded Nikola Tesla the Dr. Honoris Causa of technical science.

1942: Young King Peter II from Yugoslavia visits Dr. Nikola Tesla in his apartment in the New Yorker.

1943:On January the 7th Tesla dies quietly and alone in room #3327 on the 33rd floor of the Hotel New Yorker in New York City.
